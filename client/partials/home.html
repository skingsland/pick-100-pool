March Madness Pick 100 Tournament Pool

An exciting twist on the typical March Madness Pool, where you only pick 13 teams.

Select Your Teams

The rules for the game are simple: you pick 13 teams whose seeds add up to 100. For example, if UNC is a # 1 seed and VCU is a #13 seed and you pick them, they combine to make 14, and you need to select 11 other teams whose seeds add up to 86.

You earn points every time one of your chosen teams wins a game. If a team wins a game, you get the value of their seed plus a designated point amount, which ascends each round as follows: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32. So, if you picked VCU as a #13 seed and they win games in the first three rounds, you would get 13+1, 13+2, and 13+4.


To get started now, select a March Madness pool, and pick your 13 teams!

Some more notes:

  1. Up until the start of the first game in the "round of 64", only you can see your bracket, when you're logged in.
  2. After that, everyone else can see your bracket, and all brackets are unchangeable.
  3. The website doesn't have a way to let you select the winner of a play-in game in advance. So if you want to pick one of the play-in teams, you'll have to do it after they've won their play-in game but before the start of the tourney.
